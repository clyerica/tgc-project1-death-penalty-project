    Despite claims of the death penalty's usefulness in deterrance, there has been an increasing trend in drug abuse since 2003 both by repeat and new offenders. Although popular trends of which drug is in vogue changes over the years, minority racial groups in Singapore remain disproportionately represented amongst drug abusers. The younger generation (below 40) in Singapore made up more than 60% of Singapore's drug abusers between 2015-2020. These figures cast doubts on the actual efficacy of capital punishment in deterring drug abuses in Singapore.

    Incarceration rates for drug users that dont meet the death penalty threshold have also been increasing over the years. Between 2018 and 2021 the population of inmates in the Drug Rehabilitation Center (DRC) increased over twofold. Although the proportion of inmates over 40 has increased, so has the number of inmates who did not achieve higher education. This indicates the potential correlation between educational inequality and drug abuse

    Education inequality is not the only potential underlying factor that pushes vulnerable members of society to drug abuse. The death penalty is a cruel and inhuman punishment which discriminates against the poorest and most disadvantaged members of society. We must strive towards creating change and achieving a fairer criminal justice system, where the human rights of all people are upheld.
